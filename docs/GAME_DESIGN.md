# Game Design

Aren betrayed Lord Celium's occult faction and uses its corrupted magic to protect common people. The intended feel is a heavy, readable 16-bit action-platformer: deliberate jumps, attacks that remain available in the air, oppressive layered scenery, and short enemy-filled panels.

The 15-minute run has six rooms across three areas. Forest enemies teach movement and resource use. In the Forest Depths, Aren meets Sillius, clears a faction patrol, unlocks Chain Lightning, and gains a temporary ranged companion. The shrine locks its crypt until the Mire Priest dies. The mountain path tests the expanded enemy roster before Lord Celium's two-phase fight. Four stones improve health, mana, magic, and movement; the Old Villager's moonstone quest rewards max health.

New enemies create distinct platforming decisions: Shielded Knights hold ground, Rift Witches attack from elevated positions, and Winged Curses pressure Aren in the air. Corrupted floor hazards and multi-height platforms force movement during combat.

Combat should reward spacing: melee is free and close, magic is ranged but costs mana, dash avoids danger. Death resets the run. Primitive silhouettes and distinct colors communicate enemy roles without assets.
