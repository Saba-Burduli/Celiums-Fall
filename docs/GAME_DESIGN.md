# Game Design

Aren betrayed Lord Celium's occult faction and uses its corrupted magic to protect common people. The intended feel is a heavy, readable 16-bit action-platformer: deliberate jumps, attacks that remain available in the air, oppressive layered scenery, and short enemy-filled panels.

Lord Celium's Ashen Covenant raised a veil that drains the valley to preserve its master. Aren was the Covenant's collector until he learned the four corrupted stones powered that sacrifice. He stole the stones, abandoned the faction, and now crosses the occupied valley to destroy the Mire Ward, free the souls beneath the Ossuary, breach the Black Keep, and kill Lord Celium at the veil's heart. Sillius begins as another disillusioned servant and becomes the witness who confirms the faction's true purpose.

The run has nine rooms across three regions. Forest enemies teach movement and resource use. In the Forest Depths, Aren meets Sillius, clears a faction patrol, unlocks Chain Lightning, and gains a temporary ranged companion before crossing Thornwatch Ruins. The shrine locks its crypt until the Mire Priest dies; the Drowned Ossuary extends the descent. The mountain path and Black Keep test the expanded enemy roster before Lord Celium's two-phase fight. Four stones improve health, mana, magic, and movement; the Old Villager's moonstone quest rewards max health.

New enemies create distinct platforming decisions: Shielded Knights hold ground, Rift Witches attack from elevated positions, and Winged Curses pressure Aren in the air. Corrupted floor hazards, solid ruined columns, multi-height ledges, and moving platforms force movement during combat. Elevated gaps are tuned around Aren's 190 speed, 670 jump impulse, and 1500 gravity rather than requiring precision at the edge.

Combat should reward spacing: melee is free and close, magic is ranged but costs mana, dash avoids danger. Death resets the run. Primitive silhouettes and distinct colors communicate enemy roles without assets.
